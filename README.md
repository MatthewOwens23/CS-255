Briefly summarize the DriverPass project. Who was the client? What type of system did they want you to design?

The DriverPass project involved designing a system for DriverPass, a company that helps students prepare for DMV driving tests. The client wanted a web-based system that would allow students to register for courses, schedule driving lessons, take practice tests, and track their progress. The system also needed to support instructors, administrators, and secretaries who manage appointments, user accounts, and training materials.

What did you do particularly well?

One area I performed well in was gathering and translating business requirements into a complete system design. I created UML diagrams, process models, and design documentation that clearly showed how the system would function and how users would interact with it. I also focused on ensuring the design addressed both functional and nonfunctional requirements.

If you could choose one part of your work on these documents to revise, what would you pick? How would you improve it?

If I could revise one part of my work, I would improve the UML diagrams by adding additional detail and refining relationships between classes and system components. While the diagrams accurately represented the system, I believe additional annotations and clearer organization would make them easier for stakeholders and developers to understand.

How did you interpret the user’s needs and implement them into your system design? Why is it so important to consider the user’s needs when designing?

I interpreted the user's needs by reviewing the interview transcript and identifying the goals, challenges, and expectations described by the client. These requirements were incorporated into the system design through features such as online scheduling, practice exams, account management, and role-based access controls. Considering user needs is critical because the success of a system depends on its ability to solve real problems and provide a positive user experience.

How do you approach designing software? What techniques or strategies would you use in the future to analyze and design a system?

I approach software design by first gathering requirements and understanding the needs of stakeholders. From there, I create models such as use case diagrams, activity diagrams, and class diagrams to visualize the system before development begins. In the future, I will continue using UML modeling, requirement analysis, stakeholder interviews, and iterative design techniques to ensure systems are well planned and meet user expectations.
